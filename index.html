After you turn on your Handibot, the first thing you will typically want to do is to accurately set the 0 location for each axis. Just click this green button.

Setting the XYZ Zero Locations

The Zeroing of an axis is automatically done for you by moving into the end-stops of the axis and then backing off an exact known distance to the location where Zero for the particular axis is set.

The back-off distance is determined at the factory and saved as a persistant variable. As you get more experienced, you may want to change the back-off distance in order to move your zero locations to better suit your work, for a particular project, or just to make a slight adjustment of your zero location. You can modify the back-off distance for each axis in the Preferences tab.

Using the XYZ Zero function in this app is the same thing as clicking on Macro#3 in the system macros, where the motion instructions are stored. It also the same as using the [C3] Command in the Sb4 App or in the OpenSBP fabrication language. You can use each, interchangeably.

Your tool moves around in a Cartesean coordinate system. The X axis (right/left) and the Y axis (back/front) are in the plane of the material. X = 0 and Y = 0 are at the bottom left corner as you face the front of the tool. Moving the tool head to the right is moving in increasing value in the X axis, moving it towards the back is moving it in increasing value the Y axis. Downward movement of the Z axis, the up/down motion of the router, is negative. Zero in the Z is typically set to the height of the surface of the material, though it need not be. Measuring Bit Length

Measuring Bit Length is sometimes called Z-Zeoring, because we are really determing the zero location for the tip of the cutter in the Z axis. More importantly for a Handibot, we are determining the back-off distance from the top Z-axis stop to having the tip of the cutter exactly at the material (at Zero). Your tool uses electrical contact with a Z-zero plate to tell exactly the distance the tip of the cutter from the stop. It then adds the known distance from the Z-zero plate to the material surface in order to calculate an overall back-off distance that we call, "current_cutter_Zoffset". So it is not really a measurement of the actual length of the bit, but the determination of an offset distance that is based on the exposed length of the cutter.

You will also see the "current_cutter_Zoffset" in the Preferences tab.

Preference Settings

Your Handibot contains a number of variables with preset values that is uses in some of the operations it does to assist you. These variables all have default values that come installed with your tool, but you are able to modify them to suit your specific needs. After you change them, the values will persist even when the tool is turned off. They are called "persistent variables".
